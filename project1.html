Executive Summary

1.1 Objective

This report was produced in answer to the questions briefed by the Airbnb team in Rio:
What key metrics should be monitored over time, to measure the success of the team's efforts in improving the guest host matching process and why?
What areas should we invest in to increase the number of successful bookings in Rio de Janeiro?
There is also interest from executives at Airbnb about the work you are doing, and a desire to understand the broader framing of the challenge of matching supply and demand, thinking beyond the data provided. What other research, experiments, or approaches could help the company get more clarity on the problem?

1.3 Key Findings

  • We discovered that more than 50% of all book_it events did not result in a booking and we examined why that might be using machine learning methods. Unfortunately we were unable to ascertain a reliable pattern that provided insight to this. Instead we have recommended that further analysis be carried out and have recommended further datasets that might help with this.
  • We found that the count of reviews appeared to be a significant factor in guests propensity to book a particular property. We also found that new bookers seem to be more important as a component of a successful booking and have recommended that Airbnb focus on a post travel customer relationship
